Emergency Preparedness Spring

Emergency Preparedness Spring is an important time of year! As we transition from the winter season, it is essential to be aware of potential (natural) disasters and how to prepare for them. Neglecting to do such can result in serious consequences. We must take steps to create a plan and ensure we are well-equipped with necessary supplies.

Firstly, (it's important to) keep updated on local weather forecasts so that you're aware of any impending dangers. This will give you time to react accordingly. For example, if severe storms are expected then it would be wise to store non-perishable food items, water and a first-aid kit in case of emergencies. Additionally, having extra battery powered lights and/or candles can help should there be a power outage.

However, this isn't all that needs to be done! It's also prudent to have an evacuation route planned just incase you need to leave your home quickly or are displaced temporarily due interuptions in services. Knowing pathways out of your neighbourhood is critical as this could save vital seconds if disaster strikes suddenly. Furthermore, know where the nearest shelters are located and which ones accept pets should you have any accompanying animals during your evacuation processs.

All in all, Emergency Preparedness Spring provides us with the opportunity (to review our safety procedures). Taking appropriate measures can protect both ourselves and our loved ones should some type of natural disaster occurr! So make sure you're adequately prepared before summer arrives by taking these necessary precautions now!Emergency Preparedness Mont Belvieu

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of emergency food supplies should I have on hand for a hurricane?
Non-perishable canned and dry goods, such as beans, nuts, peanut butter, crackers, canned meat or fish, cereal, energy bars and powdered milk.
When should I stock up on hurricane emergency food supplies?
It is best to prepare an emergency food supply well in advance of the arrival of a hurricane.
How long will the hurricane emergency food supplies last?
Depending on the size of your family and how much you have stored up, your emergency food supply could last anywhere from several days to several weeks.
Where can I find reliable information about emergency preparedness for hurricanes?
The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) website provides up to date information about preparing for hurricanes and other natural disasters.
