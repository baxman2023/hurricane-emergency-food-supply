How to Store Emergency Food Supplies Properly


Don't risk going hungry! Prepare for the worst and be sure to know how to store emergency food supplies properly. Knowing what items to buy and where to store them is key in an emergency situation. Start by buying canned goods, such as vegetables, soups and fruit. These can last a long time and provide essential vitamins and minerals. Also consider buying dry goods like rice, beans, pasta, cereal, flour or crackers. Avoiding buying perishables like meat or dairy products as they won't keep long enough.

Next up: storing it all! Be sure to put all food in sealed containers that will protect against rodents or insects. Make sure the pantry is cool and dry - heat will cause food items to spoil quickly. Canned goods should also be rotated every six months so nothing goes bad (check expiration dates often!). All of this may seem like a lot of work but it's worth it! Having access to non-perishable foods when times get tough could mean the difference between going hungry or not during an emergency situation.

Lastly, always keep a 3-day supply of extra non-perishables on hand - just in case you need them! Even if there isn't an immediate threat of disaster looming over you, having some extra just lying around never hurts – better safe than sorry!
