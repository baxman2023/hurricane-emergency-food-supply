Emergency Food Supplies – What You Need to Stock Up On


Worried about another big storm coming? Investing in emergency food supplies is the best way to stay protected! (It's) not just a smart idea, it's a necessity. You want to make sure you have enough food and water to last through any potential power outages that may happen. Here's what you need:

First, non-perishable foods like canned goods, dry pasta and beans. These are easy to store and can provide important sustenance if you don't have access to fresh groceries for awhile. Don't forget ample quantities of protein-rich items like peanut butter and canned fish or meat too. Also stock up on crackers, nuts, energy bars - anything that won't spoil quickly!

Moreover, make sure you have enough clean drinking water. A gallon per person per day is recommended; fill up containers from your tap before the storm hits or buy premade bottles at the store. If you don't have access to running water during an emergency, get a few extra gallons of bottled water just in case!

Lastly, consider purchasing an emergency generator if possible. This can be useful for powering basic appliances like refrigerators and lights when the power goes out unexpectedly. Don't forget batteries too – they're essential for flashlights and radios so you can keep informed on the situation outside!

In conclusion, being prepared with emergency food supplies is key to staying protected during bad storms or natural disasters. Make sure you’ve got all of these essentials covered beforehand – this way you'll be ready for whatever comes your way!

Tips for Storing Emergency Food Supplies

Here are some tips to help you store emergency food supplies: (1) Choose non-perishable items (canned foods, grains, etc.) that will last a long time without spoiling. (4) Store supplies in a cool dark place, such as a basement or garage; this will help them stay fresher for longer periods of time too.

Additionally, don't forget to rotate your stock every six months or so! This'll ensure that the food stays safe to eat and hasn't gone bad - nothing worse than having to throw out all those goods due to spoilage! Also consider investing in freeze-dried foods or dehydrated meals which come with their own packaging and can last up to 25 years. These make great options for long term storage just incase anything drastic should happen.

How to Stay Protected During a Storm


When another big storm is headed our way, we may feel anxious and scared. But with a few preparations, we can stay safe and protected! First off, it's important to invest in emergency food supplies( like canned goods or dehydrated meals), just in case the power goes out for an extended period of time. Make sure you have enough non-perishable items to last your family up to two weeks (or more if possible). Additionally, make provisions for pets if necessary.

Also, avoid going outdoors during the storm unless absolutely necessary. If you must go outside, be sure to wear protective clothing and footwear that covers as much skin as practicable. And remember to feely check on elderly family members or neighbors who might need assistance during this time!

Furthermore, keep any exposed windows covered with plywood boards or shutters so no glass shatters from high winds or debris flying around. It's also wise to unplug all electronics before the storm begins as a precaution; this includes computers and other home appliances. Having a generator backup system can help too - just in case!

Finally, remember that storms are unpredictable and safety should always come first! Be prepared for anything that pops up by having contact information for police and fire departments nearby in case of emergencies. Also have a plan B ready just incase something goes wrong; then you'll be able to act quickly without any fuss! All these steps will ensure that both you and your loved ones remain safe throughout the storm – no matter how severe it may be!
