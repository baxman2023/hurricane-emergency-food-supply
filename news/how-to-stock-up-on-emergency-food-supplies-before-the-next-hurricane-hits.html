How to Stock Up on Emergency Food Supplies Before the Next Hurricane Hits

How to Stock Up on Emergency Food Supplies Before the Next Hurricane Hits

Posted by on 2023-08-18

Create an emergency food list


When a hurricane strikes, it can leave people stranded and without access to food. To be prepared for such an event, it is important to stock up on emergency food supplies. First, consider the types of foods that will last in an emergency situation - canned goods are a great option! Canned fruits (and veggies), soups, tuna, beans and other proteins (such as pre-cooked sausages) are good choices. It's also wise to store some grains like pastas and rice. These should be kept in airtight containers to prevent them from spoiling quickly.

Next, think about non-perishable snacks like nuts and granola bars that don't require refrigeration or cooking. Boxed milk is another smart choice; its shelf-life is much longer than regular milk! Be sure to include some condiments too - these can make canned items more appetizing as well as add variety during an extended period of time without access to fresh ingredients or restaurants.

It's also important not to forget about beverages - both alcoholic and non-alcoholic ones! Have plenty of bottled water on hand but don't neglect juices, teas and coffee either - these will help keep everyone hydrated during a storm. Plus if there's no power available you won't have to worry about making hot drinks! Lastly, don't forget pet food if needed - ensure your furry family members are taken care of too!

Overall, stocking up on emergency food supplies before the next hurricane hits requires forethought. Packing a variety of items ranging from cans of soup to boxes of cereal will help ensure everyone stays fed through any stormy weather. Moreover, remember those pet supplies so your animals remain happy and healthy too! With proper planning ahead of time you'll be ready for whatever Mother Nature throws your way!

Additionally, if you have infants at home make sure to store plenty of formula and baby food as well! And remember: it never hurts to have a bit more than necessary – just incase! Preparing ahead of time can help alleviate some of the stress associated with natural disasters like hurricanes – so prioritize these essential items now!

Research and buy from a reliable source


Stocking up on emergency food supplies is an essential part of prepping for a hurricane! With (so) much uncertainty, it's important to be prepared and have enough food in your pantry to last through the storm. To get started, research reliable sources that can provide information about stocking up on emergency foods. It's also important to consider what types of food you should buy. For instance, non-perishable items such as canned fruit and vegetables, peanut butter, crackers, cereal and nuts are all good options. Additionally, you may want to purchase extra water or juice if possible - this will help keep your family hydrated during the storm.

When purchasing emergency foods, make sure not to buy too many perishables; these can quickly go bad if there is a loss of power or refrigeration. Look for items with high nutritional value and long shelf lives - this includes things like granola bars, energy bars or dried fruits & nuts. Additionally (you should) avoid processed meats like bacon or hot dogs as they tend to spoil more quickly than other items. Lastly, check expiration dates before making your purchases so that you don't end up with expired food when the time comes to use it.

Canned goods like fruits, vegetables, and soups are also good options because they usually have a long shelf life. Dried beans, rice, nuts and seeds are other great staples; they contain protein and are relatively inexpensive.

But don't forget about beverages too! Stocking up on bottles of water is essential if you plan to ride out the storm at home.
