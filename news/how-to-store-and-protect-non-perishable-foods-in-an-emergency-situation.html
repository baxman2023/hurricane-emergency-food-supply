Gather the Necessary Supplies


Storing and protecting non-perishable foods during an emergency is critical to survival. Gatherin' the necessary supplies ahead of time will make this task much simpler! It's important to have a dry, cool place (free from rodents or pests) to store your food and other supplies. A basement or pantry is an ideal location; however, you can also use closets, garage and even cardboard boxes.

There are many tools that can help in storing and protecting non-perishable foods: airtight containers, oxygen absorbers, desiccant packets, zip lock bags, Mylar bags with oxygen absorbers as well as vacuum sealing machines. You should also consider purchasing a quality food grade storage container (with a lid) for long-term preservation of your food. This type of container will keep out moisture and insects while still allowing proper ventilation. Additionally, it's best practice to label each item so you know when it was stored and what expiration date it has (if applicable).

Another key factor when storing non-perishables is temperature control. The ideal temperature range for preserving most items is between 40°F - 70°F; otherwise bacteria may begin to develop inside the products which could lead to spoilage or contamination. To maintain consistent temperatures within your storage area, consider investing in thermometers or digital hygrometers that can track humidity levels.
Additionally, you'll want to periodically inspect your items for any signs of damage or spoilage such as mold growth on packaging or discoloration on products themselves. If these signs are present then it's best to discard any affected items immediately!

Overall, proper storage and protection of non-perishable foods during an emergency situation requires some effort but if done correctly can really make a difference in ensuring safety and freshness of your goods! With the right supplies at hand plus attention to detail you'll be able feel secure knowing that your food supply will last through whatever crisis comes its way!

In an emergency situation, choosing a cool, dark and dry place to store non-perishable foods is key. Avoiding these places is important in order to keep your food safe!

When selecting a spot for storage, aim for someplace with low humidity levels and temperatures between 50-70 degrees Fahrenheit. A basement or garage may be ideal in this case! You may want to consider investing in airtight containers and/or sealing bags so you can protect your goodies from moisture and other elements.

Furthermore, think about adding quick snacks like granola bars and fruit chews into your stockpile; these are great options if you need sustenance fast! However, don't forget about meals either; opt for canned goods that are rich in protein like beans or canned tuna. Additionally, include dishes containing plenty of healthy carbs such as oatmeal packs and whole grain cereals - these won't only keep you full but provide essential nutrients too!

In conclusion, remember to store non-perishable foods in a cool, dark & dry place during an emergency situation.
