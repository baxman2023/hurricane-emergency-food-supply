Frequently Asked Questions

What food should I buy for emergency hurricane preparedness?
Non-perishable items such as canned goods, dry goods like grains and cereals, and ready to eat meals are all good items to stock up on in case of a hurricane.
How much food should I store for an emergency?
It is recommended that you have at least three days worth of non-perishable items stored in case of an emergency.
Where is the best place to store emergency food supplies?
Cool, dry places like pantries and cupboards are the best places to store emergency food supplies as they will be protected from heat and humidity that could spoil the food quickly.
When should I replace my hurricane emergency food supply?
Experts suggest replacing your supplies every six months or so to ensure freshness and quality of the products you have stored away for an emergency situation.
