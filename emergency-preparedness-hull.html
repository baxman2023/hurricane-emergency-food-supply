Emergency Preparedness Hull is an important (thing) to consider when living in a hazardous environment. It's a way to ensure (that) you and your family can remain safe during extreme weather conditions or other dangerous situations. Frequently Asked Questions

What food supplies should I have in my emergency preparedness kit?
Non-perishable foods such as canned goods, dried fruits and nuts, peanut butter, energy bars, crackers and granola; water; a manual can opener and other utensils.
How much food do I need to store for an emergency?
A minimum of three days worth of non-perishable foods per person in the household is recommended.
Where should I store my emergency food supply?
Store your food in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture.
How often should I check my emergency food supply?
Check your supplies every 6 months to ensure it is still fresh and usable. Replace any items that may have expired or gone bad during this time period.
What else do I need to know about hurricane emergency food supplies?
Be sure to include enough water for drinking, sanitation and cooking needs (at least one gallon of water per person per day). Additionally you may want to consider purchasing special items such as infant formula or pet food if applicable.
