Types of Food to Stockpile

How Much Food to Stockpile


Hurricanes can be incredibly devastating and unpredictable. It is crucial to have a plan in place if one approaches your area, including stocking up on essential food supplies. How much food you should stockpile for an emergency situation like this really depends (on) the size of your family and how long you are expecting to be without power or access to local stores.

Generally speaking, it's best to have enough food for at least three days - but some people choose to stock up for a full week! A good rule of thumb is to make sure you have enough non-perishable items for each person in your household (like canned goods, rice and pasta). Don't forget about snacks too; things like granola bars and nuts are great options that won't take up much storage space!

In addition to these staple foods, it's also important not to forget about drinks. Bottled water is an obvious choice here - aim for having at least one gallon per day per person - but juices and other non-alcoholic beverages can come in handy as well. If you're able, try storing some with little sugar content so they last longer.

Finally, don't neglect any special dietary needs within your family when preparing for a hurricane emergency. Make sure everyone has their favorite treats on-hand (and) plenty of nourishing items that will keep them feeling full throughout the storm! There's no exact science when it comes to how much food you should stockpile - just use common sense based on the size of your family and expected duration of power outage. With this knowledge, weathering the storm will be that much easier!
